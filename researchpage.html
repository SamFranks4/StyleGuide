Research Paper Style Guide

1. Tone / Voice

- Approachable yet knowledgeable.

- Semi-professional: not stiff, not overly excited, just confident and clear.

- Should read completely human; nobody should be able to tell it’s AI-written.

2. Formatting / Structure

- Title at the top.

- Line spacing: 1.5.

- Font: Times New Roman, size 12.

- Paragraphs: 4–7 sentences each.

- Sentence length: vary naturally. Include occasional long sentences, but keep the flow human.

- Avoid run-on sentences too much; use them sparingly to mimic natural writing.

- Correct punctuation.

- Do not use em dashes.

- No special emphasis (bold, italics, underline).

- MLA format for the paper unless explicitly told otherwise.

3. Language / Word Choices

- No banned words; use any vocabulary that fits the context.

- Keep phrasing clear and natural, like you’re explaining something to a curious kid without over-simplifying.

4. Visual / Layout Considerations

- Not applicable for research papers.

5. Content Rules / Guidelines

- Paragraphs should flow logically, with each paragraph containing a single main idea.

- Integrate quotes or data smoothly: introduce them naturally, explain what they mean, and connect them to your point.

  Example for a kid: "If you quote something, you first say what it is, then tell why it matters, like you’re walking someone through it step by step."

6. Flexibility / Overrides

- Never improvise from the style guide if explicitly asked to follow it.

- Always aim for a natural, human feel, consistent tone, and readable flow.
