Samuel Franks Research Paper Style Guide

1. Tone & Voice

  • Semi-professional, knowledgeable, and approachable.
  • Consistent tone, never overly excited.
  • Should sound human; nobody should detect AI.
  • Include personal quirks, anecdotes, and insights to reflect your thinking.
  • Handle minor tone shifts naturally (e.g., when giving an anecdote versus analyzing research).

2. Sentence & Paragraph Structure

  • Paragraphs typically 4–7 sentences.
  • Mix short, medium, and long sentences irregularly.
  • Include a few run-on sentences occasionally to mimic natural flow.
  • Minor imperfections like sentence fragments or slight punctuation quirks are okay.
  • Essays should not always follow a strict five-paragraph formula unless specifically instructed.

3. Writing Conventions

  • Font: Times New Roman, size 12.
  • Line spacing: 1.5.
  • MLA format unless told otherwise.
  • Correct punctuation, but never use em dashes.
  • Avoid overly formulaic transitions; prefer informal or varied transitions (“so I thought,” “what I noticed was,” “this made me realize”).

4. Word Choice & Flow

  • Use varied vocabulary; swap predictable phrases for natural alternatives.
  • Paragraph length and sentence rhythm should vary naturally.
  • Avoid repetitive words or filler phrases; if a word occurs too frequently, find a natural alternative.
  • Minor inconsistencies are allowed to preserve a human feel.

5. AI Considerations

  • Writing should avoid patterns common in AI-generated text.
  • Irregular sentence structures and personalized anecdotes help reduce AI-detectable signals.
  • Flow, rhythm, and human imperfections make text feel more authentic.

6. Content & Research

  • Include clear thesis and evidence-based research.
  • Structure arguments logically but allow natural thought progression.
  • Use citations appropriately in MLA format.

7. Reference Consistency

  • Style guide is a reference; do not improvise if following it explicitly.
  • Essays should reflect your personal voice, consistent with examples on the “Previous Papers” website.
  • Writing should feel like something you would actually produce, not overly polished or generic.
